Dear Singaporean, are you a
Sustainable Consumer?

The Singapore Government has recently announced the SG Green Plan 2030,
a nation-wide sustainable development agenda.

As a consumer, you have a really big role to play in ensuring the success of this plan!
In your everyday life, the food and fashion products that you consumer,
as well as your recycling efforts can make a big difference to the sustainability of our nation.


Food Products

Current Habits

Since 2015, the consumption of beef in Singapore has been on an ever-rising trend.

Did you know that consuming meat, especially beef, is an extremely unsustainable practice?

Beef requires more resources to produce that most other types of meat, even when meat is already more resource-intensive to produce than plant-based foods.

Food Products

Effects

In 2020 and 2021, major meat and dairy companies had higher carbon-equivalent emissions than leading fossil fuel companies.

Meat production mostly releases methan, a greenhouse gas which traps heat in the Earth's atmosphere. Greenhouse gases work to keep our planet warm through the greehouse effect, but too much would mean global warming!
