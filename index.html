Being a sustainable consumer would entail consuming resources and products in a sustainable and responsible way,
such that both current and future needs can be met, without creating a negative impact on the environment.



The Singapore Government has recently announced the SG Green Plan 2030,
a nation-wide sustainable development agenda.

As a consumer, you have a really big role to play in ensuring the success of this plan!
In your daily lives, the food and fashion products that you consume,
as well as your recycling efforts can make a big difference to the sustainability of our nation.

Read on to find out how you current consumption habits impact the environment,
as well as learn some ways you can be a more sustainable consumer!

Food Products

Current Habits

Since 2015, the consumption of beef in Singapore has been on an ever-rising trend.

Did you know that consuming meat, especially beef, is an extremely unsustainable practice?

Beef requires more resources to produce that most other types of meat, even when meat is already more resource-intensive to produce than plant-based foods.

Food Products

Effects

In 2020 and 2021, 15 major meat and dairy companies had higher carbon-equivalent emissions than leading fossil fuel companies.

Cow farts and burps mostly contain methane, a greenhouse gas which traps heat in the Earth's atmosphere. Producing more beef or cow products will lead to increased emittance of methane.
Greenhouse gases work to keep our planet warm through the greehouse effect, but too much would mean global warming!

Fashion Products

Current Habits

In terms of fashion products like cosmetics, clothing, and footwear, Singaporeans would rather switch brands than to pay for more eco-friendly products.

This causes us to support less eco-friendly fashion brands in the long-term. Our society today is plagued with fast-fashion brands and most of us are guilty of patronising these brands (e.g. Shein, Zara).




chart source

Fashion Products

Effects

The apparel industry worldwide is expected to produce 50% more carbon emissions by 2030 from 2019. This also means that its carbon emissions will rise yearly from now.

Fashion products generate carbon emissions in various parts of its life cycle, from its production to when it is discarded and ends up in landfills or incineration plants. These carbon emissions will lead to an enhanced greenhouse effect in our planet as well!

Recycling Efforts

Only a minority (28%) of households in Singapore recycle their waste.
This is a lot lower than what is ideal.

Recycling is part of the SG Green Plan 2030 'Sustainable Living' pillar. It supports Singapore's transition to become a zero waste nation. With more people engaged in recycling, it reduces the need for natural resource extraction as present materials could be reused for longer.

How can you be a sustainable consumer?

How can you
consume food products
more sustainably?

1. Reduce meat consumption


2. Switch to plant-based proteins
(e.g. tofu, soy products)


3. Become vegan if possible!

How can you
consume fashion products
more sustainably?

1. Don't engage in fast fashion
(quality over quantity)


2. Upcycle your clothes


3. Donate preloved clothes

What can you do to improve
your recycling habits?

1. Make sure the item is
clean, empty and dry


2. Avoid buying non-recyclable materials


3. Say no to plastic
