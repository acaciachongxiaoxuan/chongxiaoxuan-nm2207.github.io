Dear Singaporean, are you a Sustainable Consumer?

The Singapore Government has recently announced the SG Green Plan 2030, a nation-wide sustainable development agenda.
As a consumer, you have a really big role to play in ensuring the success of this plan!
In your everyday life, the food and fashion products that you consumer, as well as your recycling efforts can make a big difference to the sustainability of our nation.

Click on the leaf below to learn more about how you can be sustainable consumer!

Food Products

Since 2015, the consumption of beef in Singapore has been on an ever-rising trend.

Did you know that consuming meat, especially beef, is an extremely unsustainable practice?
Beef requires more resources to produce that most other types of meat, even when meat is already more
resource-intensive to produce than plant-based foods.

data source: https://www.sfa.gov.sg/docs/default-source/tools-and-resources/yearly-statistics/per-capita-consumption.pdf


Just a mere 15 meat and dairy companies are able to beat leading fossil fuel companies in 2020 and 2021 in terms of carbon emissions.

data source: https://www.iatp.org/sites/default/files/2022-11/Emissions%20Impossible_Methane%20Edition_%20FINAL.pdf

What can you do to reduce carbon emissions from meat production?

1. Reduce meat consumption
2. Opt for plant-based food options
